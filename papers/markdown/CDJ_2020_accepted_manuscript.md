Conclusions
Conducting participatory action research based on transductive reasoning
allows researchers to explore the spatial products that lead towards the
materialization of the ideas related to a community’s future. It provides
both an image of a future space and a collective deliberative process for
makingspatialdecisionsthatshapethatspace.Inthiscase,themasterplan
represents those decisions and is composed by the community with the
assistanceofexperts.Ontheonehand,themasterplanhastechnicalvalue
fordiscussingwiththeauthoritieshowthepeopleofLosArenaleswantto
live in the future. On the other hand, it is a record that explains how the
spacecanbeorganizedcollaboratively.Theuseoftransductivereasoningin
participatoryactionresearchcouldguideurbanplanningprocesseswithin
a grassroots society, not as a pragmatic approach to housing needs but as
animaginativeexplorationofwhatispossible.InLosArenales,imaginative
exploration entails the visualization of a community in which the right to
the city has been achieved. By knowing the desired outcome, the path to
thatoutcomeiseasiertotrack.

has several positive outcomes: (i) it invites dwellers living in a precarious
situation to imagine a better future, to embrace the possibility of making
it real, and to valorize each step towards the utopian idea of living better
(which, in reality, is not so utopian); (ii) as part of the efforts towards that
